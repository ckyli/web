Format of your report:

The format of the Mycoplasma testing report that you will receive is a single-sided page with a brief overview of the Materials and Methods. The report lists the Mycoplasma status (e.g. free from infection or contaminated). Cropped, electronically-labelled gel electrophoresis photographs will be on the report with two Mycoplasma positive and negative controls, along with your samples.

Treating Mycoplasma-contaminated cultures with Plasmocin:

Plasmocin is a reagent which can be ued to eliminate and prevent Mycoplasma contamination in cell cultures. Addition of Plasmocin to liquid media can prevent Mycoplasma and other general bacterial contamination in small and large animal cell cultures.

This well-established anti-Mycoplasma reagent comprises two bacteriocidal components, which are strongly active against Mycoplasmas, and enable their elimination in only 2 weeks. One component acts on the protein synthesis machinery whilst the other acts on DNA replication. These two precise and distinct targets are found only in Mycoplasma and many other bacteria, and are absent from eukaryotic cells.
